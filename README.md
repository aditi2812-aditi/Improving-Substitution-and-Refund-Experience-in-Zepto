Overview

This case study analyzes user friction in Zepto’s post-order experience, focusing on item substitutions and refund handling. The goal was to understand how these issues impact user satisfaction and identify product improvements to reduce drop-offs.

Problem

Users often receive substituted items without consent and are refunded via wallet credits instead of their original payment method. This reduces user control and creates dissatisfaction after order completion.

Study Approach

Conducted a structured study with 30 users to understand how substitution and refund experiences affect user satisfaction and continued usage.

Participants were presented with different scenarios involving:

Item substitution
Refund methods

The study was used to identify patterns in dissatisfaction, preferences, and likelihood to continue using the platform.

Key Insights
A majority of users experienced item substitution, with many reporting dissatisfaction
Most users preferred refunds to their original payment method over wallet credits
Complaints were primarily driven by incorrect items and refund-related issues
Users indicated a higher likelihood of discontinuing usage after a poor experience
Analysis

A funnel view of the post-order journey shows that substitution often leads to dissatisfaction and escalation through complaints or refunds, resulting in drop-off before retention.

Users expect reliable delivery and fair refund handling. Unexpected substitutions and inflexible refund policies break these expectations and reduce trust.

Solution

Proposed product improvements:

Allow users to opt in/out of substitutions
Enable selection of acceptable alternatives
Offer refunds to the original payment method, with wallet as an optional choice
Notify users before confirming substitutions
Prioritization

Highest priority:

Substitution control
Flexible refund options

These directly address the main sources of dissatisfaction with relatively low implementation complexity.

Validation Approach

Proposed A/B testing:

Control: current experience
Treatment: improved substitution and refund flows

Metrics:

Retention
Repeat usage
User satisfaction
Key Takeaway

Improving user control and transparency in the post-order experience can reduce friction, increase trust, and support better retention.

Note

This analysis is based on a limited sample (n=30) and should be interpreted as directional insights rather than precise measurements.
